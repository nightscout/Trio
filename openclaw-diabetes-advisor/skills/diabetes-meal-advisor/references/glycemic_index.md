# Glycemic Index Reference for Pre-Bolus Timing

Primary source: Frontiers in Nutrition tDNA-ME (PMC9235861, 2022) — most comprehensive
GI tables for Middle Eastern foods. Secondary: International GI Tables, USDA.

## GI Hierarchy for SY Foods (bolus timing implications)

### LOW GI (< 40) — Standard bolus at meal start or slightly after
- Hummus: GI **6** (lowest GI food in the SY table)
- Chickpeas (cooked): GI **28**
- Figs (fresh): GI **35**
- Ful medames (fava beans): GI **40**

### LOW-MEDIUM GI (40-55) — Standard bolus timing, no rush to pre-bolus
- Dates: GI **42** (but high sugar content causes fast spike despite low GI — treat as FAST)
- Bulgur wheat: GI **46-55** (kibbeh, tabbouleh, itch/eech)
- Sambousak dough: GI **~55** (flour-based, moderate)
- Lentils (cooked): GI **29-32**

### MEDIUM GI (55-69) — Pre-bolus 10-15 min
- Semolina-based desserts: GI **54-66** (adjwe, ma'amoul, basbousa base)
- Knafeh: GI **~62**
- Baklawa: GI **~65**
- Syrian flatbread: GI **~68**
- Hamod soup (with rice): GI **~68**

### HIGH GI (70+) — Pre-bolus 15-20+ min when BG is in range
- White rice / riz: GI **70-85** (fastest-spiking SY staple)
- Ka'ak bread ring: GI **~72**
- Challah: GI **~75**
- Sahlab: GI **~75**
- Honey: GI **87**

## Composite Meal Effects

**Riz bi sha'riyeh (rice with vermicelli):** The ~10-15% vermicelli component (wheat
pasta, GI ~35) modestly lowers overall GI compared to plain white rice, but the effect
is small. Still treat as HIGH GI. (UNVERIFIED: theoretical estimate, not lab-tested.)

**Legumes + high-GI foods:** When hummus, ful, or chickpeas are eaten alongside
higher-GI foods (rice, bread), they help moderate glucose spikes through the "second
meal effect" of fiber and protein. This is clinically documented.

**High-fat meals:** Fat delays gastric emptying regardless of GI. A high-fat mezze
spread with hummus, tahini, and fried foods will absorb slower than the raw GI values
suggest. Consider extending the bolus or using a split bolus for high-fat + high-GI
combinations (e.g., fried kibbeh + challah).

## Pre-Bolus Timing Guide (BG-dependent)

### BG in range (80-120 mg/dL)
| Food GI | Pre-bolus time |
|---------|---------------|
| LOW (< 40) | At meal start or slightly after |
| LOW-MEDIUM (40-55) | 0-10 min before |
| MEDIUM (55-69) | 10-15 min before |
| HIGH (70+) | 15-20 min before |

### BG slightly high (120-160 mg/dL)
Add 5-10 min to all timings above.

### BG high (> 160 mg/dL)
Add 10-20 min. For HIGH GI foods, consider bolusing 30+ min before eating.

### BG low or dropping (< 80 mg/dL)
Eat first. Bolus after eating or reduce dose. For LOW GI foods, may skip bolus
reduction. For HIGH GI foods, still bolus but can do so 10-15 min into the meal.

### BG dropping fast (↓↓)
Eat first, reassess BG after 15 min, then bolus with 10-20% reduction.
